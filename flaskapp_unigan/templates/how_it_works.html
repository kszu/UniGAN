How does UniGAN work?

UniGAN is built using what’s called a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN). GAN’s were first created in 2014 by Ian Goodfellow, and have seen a number of applications and developments in the subsequent years.

At their core, GAN’s allow synthetic data to be generated similarly to the data seen in the training set. For instance, in the case of UniGAN, by training our GAN on tens of thousands of shoe images, we’re able to generate novel unisex product designs within seconds.

To learn more about UniGAN, other GAN technologies, and our inspiration, please visit our GitHub repository.
